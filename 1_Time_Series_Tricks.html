Time Series

Time series data, especially sensor water quality data is frequently gappy, prone to sensor errors, and can be unintuitive to work with. Here we will walk through some water quality data from the Kansas River in Topeka, Kansas. This is an excellent site for exploring water quality data because it is a USGS Super gage where they measure discharge, temp, do, chlorophyll a, salinity, and turbidity.

  fill_gaps()

Imputing over the gaps

chl_gap_filled <- chl_full %>%
  mutate(impute_lin = na_interpolation(chl_a_Inst, 'linear'),
         impute_spline = na_interpolation(chl_a_Inst, 'spline'),
         impute_kalman = na_kalman(chl_a_Inst),
         impute_locf = na_locf(chl_a_Inst)) %>%
  pivot_longer(chl_a_Inst:impute_locf, names_to = 'imputation_method')



ggplot(chl_gap_filled, aes(x = dateTime, y=value, color = imputation_method,
                           size = imputation_method)) + 
         geom_line() +
  scale_size_manual(values = c(2,1.5,1,0.5,0.25)) + 
  ylim(0,50)
